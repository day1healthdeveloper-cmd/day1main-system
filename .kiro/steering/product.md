# Product Overview

Day1Main is a comprehensive medical insurance operating system for South African medical schemes. The platform manages the complete lifecycle of health insurance operations including member enrollment, claims processing, provider network management, and regulatory compliance.

## Core Domains

**Member Management**: Handles member enrollment, dependant management, policy administration, and family structures. Supports 4,700+ members with proper dependant codes and broker associations.

**Claims Processing**: Full claims adjudication system with support for direct claims (provider-submitted), refund claims (member-submitted), pharmacy claims (point-of-sale), and hospital pre-authorization. Designed to meet the statutory 30-day payment requirement.

**Provider Network**: Manages 1,900+ healthcare providers including GPs, specialists, hospitals, pharmacies, and allied health professionals. Supports HPCSA registration verification and PCNS practice number validation.

**Application System**: Multi-step onboarding flow with OCR-powered document processing (ID documents, proof of address), selfie verification, medical history collection, and digital signature capture.

**Compliance**: Built for South African Medical Schemes Act No. 131 of 1998 compliance, including Prescribed Minimum Benefits (PMBs), chronic disease list management, and Council for Medical Schemes (CMS) oversight requirements.

## Key Features

- Multi-department access (Admin, Call Centre, Claims Assessor, Compliance, Finance, Operations, Marketing, Broker)
- Provider portal for claims submission and payment tracking
- Member portal for claim tracking and benefit checking
- OCR document processing using Google Cloud Vision API
- Fraud detection and prevention system
- Real-time eligibility checking
- ICD-10 diagnosis code validation
- Tariff code management

## Regulatory Context

The system addresses South Africa's R22-28 billion annual fraud, waste, and abuse (FWA) problem in the medical scheme industry while ensuring full regulatory compliance with CMS requirements.
